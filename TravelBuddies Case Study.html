the gap

Every competitor forces a tradeoff: book first, then meet (HostelWorld); match randomly, then go quiet (Travello, GAFFL); or feel unsafe and outdated (Tourbar, Couchsurfing). None combine safe verification with real, filterable compatibility for travel companions specifically.

meet the users

User Personas & Journeys

To better understand our target users, we examined their attitudes, motivations, goals, and pain points, then mapped each to a corresponding journey.

Meticulous Marissa

A 29-year-old CEO based in Toronto, Canada. As a highly detail-oriented planner, she enjoys mapping out trips well in advance to ensure a smooth experience. While she loves traveling, she prefers having companions who are reliable, organized, and aligned with her itinerary. She's looking for like-minded travelers who appreciate structure but are still open to adventure.

Pain Points

  • Dislike for Chaos: Gets frustrated with last-minute changes or disorganized travel plans.
  • Finding Like-Minded Planners: Struggles to find travel companions who appreciate structured itineraries.
  • Planning Fatigue: Feels exhausted being the only one managing logistics in group trips.

Needs

  • Compatible Travel Partners: A way to connect with travel buddies who align with her planning style.
  • Clear Expectations: A way to communicate and set planning expectations with future travel buddies.
  • Reliability Checks: A rating or review system to confirm a travel buddy's dependability.
Outgoing Ollie

A 26-year-old marketing professional in Chicago. As an energetic and extroverted traveler, he thrives on meeting new people and immersing himself in new experiences. Whether exploring hidden gems or bonding with locals over food, he seeks open-minded companions who embrace adventure. While he enjoys solo trips, he finds that traveling with like-minded people makes the experience more exciting and memorable.

Pain Points

  • Difficult to Plan: Scheduling trips is challenging with varying budgets and timelines.
  • Finding the Right Vibe: Struggles to find travel companions who match his social energy and spontaneity.
  • Safety Concerns: Worries about meeting strangers in unfamiliar places.

Needs

  • Seamless Trip Planning: A way to coordinate budgets and travel days effortlessly.
  • Stress-Free Travel: Prefers relaxing over following a strict schedule.
  • Safe and Easy Connections: Wants a simple and secure way to find compatible travel partners.
Skeptical Sally

A 22-year-old undergraduate student eager to explore new places with a travel buddy over summer break. While she's excited about the idea of meeting new people, she has only ever traveled with close friends and feels uncertain about planning a trip with someone she doesn't know. She worries about compatibility, safety, and how well they will get along during the trip, making her hesitant to step outside her comfort zone.

Pain Points

  • Hard to Connect: Finds it difficult to open up to strangers.
  • Safety Concerns: Worried about the risks of traveling one-on-one with someone she doesn't know.
  • Fear of Being Stood Up: Unsure if her travel buddy will follow through on the planned trip.

Needs

  • Breaking the Ice: An active and guided conversation to help connect with new travel buddies.
  • Building Trust: Identity confirmation to ensure the travel buddy is reliable.
  • Trip Commitment: A way to confirm travel plans and prevent last-minute bailing.

From Insight to Interface

Each core area traces back to a specific research finding, not just a feature we thought would be nice to have.

01Onboarding

Build trust before the first match.

Insight

Users are looking for trustworthy depictions of other user personalities to assess compatibility and safety before traveling with others.

  • Languages & profile photos
    Shared languages and multiple representative photos give users real signal about who they'd actually be traveling with.
  • Interests & compatibility quiz
    An optional quiz surfaces travel-style compatibility upfront, rather than users guessing from a bio alone.
  • ID verification
    Verifying identity directly addresses the safety concerns users raised about traveling with strangers.
02Home

Search by person or by place.

Insight

Users typically have a set preference between searching by other users or by location, having both allows for more ways to connect.

  • Search with intent
    Users can search for specific travel partners or specific destinations, not just one fixed path.
  • Personalized top picks & filters
    Recommendations and filters by location, interests, age, and gender help users refine toward the buddies most likely to be a fit.
03Trips

Give the app one clear job.

Insight

Users want the app focused on finding travel buddies, without distractions or overwhelming features and components.

  • Upcoming & past trips
    Trips stay organized around the one thing the app is for, planning and remembering travel with a buddy.
  • Suggested trip ideas
    Destinations, budgets, and activities are suggested rather than requiring users to plan from a blank page.
  • Create & invite
    Creating a trip and adding scheduled activities keeps coordination inside the app instead of scattering across texts.
04Profile

Make credibility visible at a glance.

Insight

Users are looking for trustworthy depictions of other personalities to assess compatibility and safety before committing to travel together.

Decision

Profiles surface pronouns, languages, and travel style alongside linked social accounts, trip and buddy counts, and postcards from past trips, giving users real evidence to judge compatibility from, not just a bio.

05Chats

Give conversations a reason to start.

Insight

Users value an active user base that shares the same goals of looking for a travel buddy with similar habits.

  • Messaging & recommended buddies
    New buddy recommendations keep the app encouraging fresh connections, not just idle chats.
  • Shared interests, surfaced
    Highlighting shared interests inside a chat gives two strangers an easy, concrete way to start talking.
